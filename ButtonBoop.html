Button Boop

Introduction

I have a dog named Gandalf. He's not a very smart dog, but he is mine and he is very important to me.

I'm working on a phone application that rewards Gandalf (or any other dog/cat/player) with a click. The click, of course, means he gets a treat.

The Game

At the moment the program has two modes. The first, "Blue Boop", presents a blue screen to the dog. The dog earns a treat everytime he touches the screen.

This is the screen for "Blue Boop". Gandalf earned eight treats this time around. I didn't give him much time to play.

This is the screen for the "Button Boop" game. This was Gandalf's first time playing and he scored better than chance. I'm a very proud owner. At the same time, he really didn't earn much more than chance, so I'll hold off on sumbitting his name to the MacArthur Foundation until I've collected more data.

Next Steps

The program is still in its infancy and there are a lot of improvements I want to make

  1. Fix the display so it looks as good on the iPad as the iPhone
  2. Tweak the overall appearance to make it look more professional
  3. Add a dog-proof way to exit the game, like a twisting gesture, so he can't accidentally hit the exit button
  4. Keep track of data and display progress in a chart
  5. Buid an automatic treat dispenser, so I don't have to give him the treat.
  6. Add dificulty settlings that change the size of the box or move its location
  7. Introduce distractions like other colored boxes, or blue circles and triangles
  8. Create a new game that reqards the dog for picking the right letter, or hitting boxes in the right order

Learning Swift

This was my first venture into iOS programming so there was a very steep learning curve. xCode did not strike me as very learner-friendly, so a lot went into every small step, from getting a sound to play, to switching into and out of the games, to just getting the program to run on my phone. And getting it onto the iPad was a whole other adventure.

Despite the high levels of frustruation, I'm glad I undertook this project. Every other programing language I studied started from the same place: what is a variable? what are floats, ints, bools, and strings? what about loops and conditionals? how do you read/write to a file? and so on. While the orthography changed, the grammar was overwhelmingly consistent.

Learning Swift took me far far away from the "fors" and "ifs" and "arrays". It was more like being thrown in the deep end learning to tread water. I don't understand what everything means, not by a long shot, but I'm making it work for me and that's something.

Plus, this is the first useful program I've made. It's rewarding to see Gandalf get excited when I show him the blue screen, and also to see his sad face when he realizes play time is over.
