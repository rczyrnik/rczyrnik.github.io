It's nice knowing who our users are, but I'd really like to know if the app is successful at matching roommates together. Unfortunately, we don't know if people who meet through the app ever later on get an apartment together, but we do know who messages whom.

Some Background Stats

I looked at just over 11,000 messages sent over a 9 month range, of which only about 6% received a response. This made me curious about why the response rate was so low. If I were a user of this app, I wouldn't be happy to hear that just 1 out of every 20 messages I sent would get an answer.

I decided to investigate the question: "Why do some messages get responses and others don't?"

The Data

I needed to rearrange my data to ask my question in a machine readable way. Some possible factors come directly from the message itself (how long is it?, when was it sent?). Other factors come from the two users in the conversation (how old are they?, when do they need a room?). And the last group of factors comes from interactions between the users and the message (How similar are the users in age? How long between when the message was sent will the recipient need a room?).

The Targets

I ran my analyses two ways. First I tried to predict whether a first message would get a response. Then I reframed the question to predict the length of a conversation given information about the first message.

I already had the length of the conversation, so I just made one last column called "response?" and set that to 0 if the conversation was just one message long, and 1 if it was longer than 1 message.
