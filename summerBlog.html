26 June 2017

I quit my job yesterday.

It feels strange to be able to write that here. I've been contemplating this decision for a long time, but until now I've been hesitant to say anything online.

Looking back, it feels so inevitable. It's hard to believe I was having doubts just last week. But now it's done, and I'm so excited to embark on the next stage in my life.

The phrase "I quit my job" sounds so negative, but I don't mean it that way at all. I've had a wonderful time teaching, and I'm going to miss the challenges and rewards there, as well as the teachers I've met. But it's time to move on.

So what have I been up to?

Mostly applying to programs and studying for their interviews. Not much I can show off on this site, especially since I doubt they'd be too keen on my publishing the solutions to their questions.

I'm most proud about learning SQL. I remember starting just a month ago and thinking that it was the weirdest language I've ever seen. I still think it's pretty odd, but I've gotten the hang of it. I recently did one program's SQL self-test and breezed through it, so major kudos to Codeacademy for its excellent preparation.

I've also been studying a lot of statistics. Some of that's been working through textbooks, and some of it's been reinventing pieces from scratch. A lot of what I've learned has been through answering the questions posed in interviews and problem sets.

And I shouldn't forget working through first year calculus. I've missed that stuff!

There also have been some small fun projects.

I'm working on a collaboration with my friend Amy. She's a fantastic artist who brings math and probability into her art, creating canvases of colored squares based on dice rolls. So I'm coming at it from the other direction, creating the same type of design as Amy, but with the computer instead of paints.

It's a new way of thinking about what the computer does. I usually look for the most efficient solution, but for this project I purposefully designed a convoluted process to fit my friend's constraints.
